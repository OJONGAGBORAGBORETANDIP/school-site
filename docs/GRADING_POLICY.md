# Grading Policy: Assessment and Final Grades

This document describes how continuous assessment and final examination marks are combined to produce a total score and a letter grade for each subject. It is intended for use in school policy and reporting.

---

## 1. Assessment Components

| Component | Maximum marks | Description |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Continuous Assessment (CA)** | 30 | Quizzes, assignments, class work, and other term work. |
| **Final examination** | 70 | End-of-term written (or practical) examination. |
| **Total** | **100** | Sum of CA and examination. No further conversion is applied. |

---

## 2. Calculation Method

- **CA** is marked **out of 30**.
- The **final exam** is marked **out of 70**.
- The **final score** for the subject is:

  **Final score = CA + Exam**

- The total is already out of 100, so **no weighting or percentage conversion** is applied. Both components are added directly.

---

## 3. Grading Scale (A–F)

Grades are assigned from the total score (out of 100) using the following scale:

| Total score (out of 100) | Percentage range | Grade | Remark   |
|--------------------------|------------------|-------|----------|
| 80 – 100                 | 80% – 100%       | A     | Excellent|
| 70 – 79                  | 70% – 79%        | B     | Very Good|
| 60 – 69                  | 60% – 69%        | C     | Good     |
| 50 – 59                  | 50% – 59%        | D     | Credit   |
| 40 – 49                  | 40% – 49%        | E     | Pass     |
| 0 – 39                   | 0% – 39%         | F     | Fail     |

---

## 4. Worked Example

| Item        | Value |
|------------|--------|
| CA score   | 24 (out of 30) |
| Exam score | 56 (out of 70) |
| **Final score** | **24 + 56 = 80** (out of 100) |
| **Final grade** | **A** (Excellent) |

Explanation: 24 + 56 = 80. The total 80 falls in the range 80–100, so the grade is **A** and the remark is **Excellent**.

---

## 5. Summary

- **CA**: 0–30 marks  
- **Exam**: 0–70 marks  
- **Final score**: CA + Exam (0–100)  
- **Grade**: A–F from the table above, based on the total score only.

No further formula or conversion is used. This policy applies to all subjects unless otherwise stated in the school’s assessment guidelines.
